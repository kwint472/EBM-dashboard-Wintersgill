## Study Selection Criteria

### Inclusion Criteria
**Study Design (Priority Order):**
1. **Meta-analyses** - Highest priority for evidence synthesis
2. **Systematic reviews** - Second priority for comprehensive overviews
3. **Longitudinal studies** - For tracking burnout changes over time
4. **Cross-sectional studies** - For current state assessment (if above unavailable)

**Population Requirements:**
- Studies including healthcare workers (nurses, physicians, support staff)
- Adult healthcare professionals (18+ years)
- Studies conducted in or including United States healthcare settings
- Sample size â‰¥ 50 participants (for adequate statistical power)

**Temporal Requirements:**
- **Baseline studies:** Published 2015-2019, data collected 2010-2019
- **Impact studies:** Published 2020-2025, data collected 2020-2022
- **Intervention studies:** Published 2021-2025, data collected during or after 2020

**Content Requirements:**
- Primary focus on burnout, emotional exhaustion, or occupational stress in healthcare settings
- Leadership interventions or leadership styles as primary focus
- Quantitative burnout measurement (validated instruments preferred)
- Clear methodology and statistical analysis

### Exclusion Criteria
- Case studies or qualitative-only studies (will be captured in practitioner evidence)
- Non-peer reviewed sources (grey literature)
- Studies focused solely on non-healthcare populations
- Studies without US healthcare participants or data
- Opinion pieces, editorials, or commentary articles
- Studies with n < 50 participants
- Studies not addressing leadership or burnout relationship

## Quality Appraisal Framework

### Evidence Hierarchy (Peterson Framework)
**Level 1 - Highest Quality:**
- Meta-analyses of randomized controlled trials
- Systematic reviews with meta-analysis

**Level 2 - High Quality:**
- Individual randomized controlled trials
- Systematic reviews without meta-analysis

**Level 3 - Moderate Quality:**
- Controlled studies without randomization
- Longitudinal cohort studies

**Level 4 - Lower Quality:**
- Cross-sectional studies
- Case-control studies

### Study Quality Assessment Criteria
**For each selected study, evaluate:**

1. **Methodological Rigor:**
   - Sample size adequacy and power calculation
   - Validated burnout measurement instruments
   - Appropriate statistical analysis
   - Control for confounding variables

2. **Relevance Assessment:**
   - Population alignment with research question
   - Outcome measures relevant to burnout
   - Temporal alignment with research timeframe
   - Geographic relevance to US context

3. **Risk of Bias Evaluation:**
   - Selection bias (sampling methodology)
   - Measurement bias (instrument validity)
   - Confounding variables addressed
   - Statistical analysis appropriateness
